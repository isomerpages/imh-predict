<details class="isomer-details">
<summary>Goal #1</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p><strong><u>To Gain Deeper Insight into the Prognosis of Depressive Disorders<br></u></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The study will track a cohort of individuals diagnosed with depression
and sub-syndromal depression over 5 years.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Information will be collected on age, employment status, social networks,
psychological status, comorbidities, and baseline severity of symptoms.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The study aims to identify high-risk subgroups for persistent symptoms,
guide early identification of this at-risk group and inform targeted clinical
interventions.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</details>
<details class="isomer-details">
<summary>Goal #2</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p><strong><u>Integrating Bio-Psycho-Social Paradigms for Comprehensive Understanding</u></strong>
</p>
<p></p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Neurobiological factors</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Neurobiological markers examined will include Brain-Derived Neurotrophic
Factor</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>(BDNF) and longitudinal cortisol levels via hair samples.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Inflammatory markers like C-reactive protein (CRP), and tumor necrosis
factor-alpha (TNF-Î±) will be monitored.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Psychosocial determinants</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The study will explore the association of psychosocial factors including
age of onset, mastery, loneliness, resilience, social support and stressful
life events with depression.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Physical Health</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Body weight and height measurement, hand-grip strength assessment and
routine metabolic function tests (e.g., blood glucose, lipid profiles)
will be measured to determine the development of comorbid physical health
conditions in this population as well as the impact of depression on these
conditions.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</details>
<details class="isomer-details">
<summary>Goal #3</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p><strong><u>Determining the Outcomes of Depression</u></strong>
</p>
<p></p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The findings of the study will provide insights into recovery trajectories,
risks of relapse, and persistence of subthreshold symptoms in the Singapore
context.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The study will include participants from both tertiary care (Institute
of Mental Health, IMH) and primary care settings, this multi-site approach
will enable comparison of outcomes and care trajectories across the two
settings.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</details>
<details class="isomer-details">
<summary>Goal #4</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p><strong><u>Prevention of Depression</u></strong>
</p>
<p></p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The study will focus on subthreshold depression (SD), a significant risk
factor for major depressive disorder (MDD).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Leveraging the population sample from SMHS and HELIOS, the research will
identify individuals with subsyndromal symptoms and trial two non-pharmaceutical
interventions:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Chatbot interventions and Behavioral Activation with Mindfulness (BAM)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Internet-delivered Skills Training in Affective and Interpersonal Regulation
(i-STAIR).</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</details>
